ARTICLE 5 - PROHIBITED PRACTICES:
The following AI practices shall be prohibited:
(a) Placing on the market, putting into service or using an AI system that deploys subliminal techniques
(b) Placing on the market, putting into service or using an AI system that exploits vulnerabilities
(c) Placing on the market, putting into service or using an AI system for social scoring
(d) Using real-time remote biometric identification in publicly accessible spaces for law enforcement

ARTICLE 6 - HIGH-RISK CLASSIFICATION:
AI systems shall be considered high-risk if they meet the following criteria:
1. The AI system is intended to be used as a safety component of a product
2. The AI system is itself a product covered by EU legislation requiring third-party conformity assessment
3. The AI system is listed in Annex III

ANNEX III - HIGH-RISK AI SYSTEMS:
1. Biometric identification and categorisation of natural persons
2. Management and operation of critical infrastructure
3. Education and vocational training
4. Employment, workers management and access to self-employment
   - Recruitment and selection of persons
   - Decisions on promotion and termination
   - Task allocation and monitoring
5. Access to essential services
   - Creditworthiness assessment
   - Risk assessment and pricing for life and health insurance
   - Emergency services dispatch
6. Law enforcement
   - Individual risk assessment for crime
   - Polygraphs and similar tools
   - Evaluation of reliability of evidence
   - Predicting occurrence or reoccurrence of crimes
7. Migration, asylum and border control
8. Administration of justice and democratic processes
